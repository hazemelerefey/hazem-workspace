## 2. About Section

Copy this exactly (or tweak to taste). It's under 2,000 characters.

```
I spend most of my time digging through data to find the story hiding in it.

Right now I'm focused on e-commerce analytics, which is where I get to do the thing I enjoy most: figuring out why customers behave the way they do, then building dashboards and models that make those patterns obvious to everyone on the team.

My day-to-day tools are Python, SQL, and Power BI. I write queries to pull the data, clean it up with Pandas, run statistical tests or train ML models when the question calls for it, and then build interactive dashboards so stakeholders can actually use what I found. I've also been going deep into Agentic AI lately, designing autonomous agents that can handle repetitive analytical tasks on their own.

What makes me a little different from most data analysts is that I came up through frontend development and design before landing in data. I spent a year and a half in an intensive Udacity program learning React and Next.js, then worked as a freelance designer building brand identities and UI prototypes. That background means I care about how insights are presented, not just whether they're correct. If a dashboard is ugly or confusing, nobody's going to use it.

My portfolio site (hazemelerefy.vercel.app) is a good example of how I think. I built it with Next.js and gave it a Harry Potter-inspired design because I wanted it to feel like something you'd actually remember. Same energy I bring to data work: make it real, make it clear, make it stick.

I'm always open to interesting projects in e-commerce, retail analytics, or anything involving AI agents. Drop me a message.
```

**Why this works (avoid-ai-writing audit):**
- No "passionate about" or "driven professional"
- No "leverage" or "utilize" or "robust"
- No em-dash chains or bullet-heavy formatting
- Reads like a person talking, not a template
- Shows personality (Harry Potter reference) without being unprofessional
- Ends with a clear call to action, not a generic "let's connect"
